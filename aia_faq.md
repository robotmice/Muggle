# AIA General FAQs

Explore the frequently asked questions about AIA's programs, membership, and more.

## Membership

### What benefits does the AIA offer?
Browse our member benefits guide for a complete list of benefits, professional resources, and exclusive discounts.

### Who is eligible for AIA membership?
Here is an overview of AIA's member categories:
- **Architect:** Individuals who are currently entitled under law to practice architecture and use the title architect in any state of the United States.
- **Associate (non-international):** Individuals without architectural licenses from a U.S. authority who meet specific requirements (e.g., eligible by education/experience, employed under an architect, have professional degrees, or are faculty members).
- **International Associates:** Individuals without architectural licenses from a U.S. licensing authority who have an architectural license or equivalent from a non-U.S. licensing authority.

### Do you have a membership level for companies?
Not at the national level. Your firm may choose to become a Cornerstone Partner or a Continuing Education Provider. Local chapters may offer additional options.

### Can I purchase AIA membership for multiple employees at once?
Yes. Through the AIA Group Enrollment Program, architecture firms with 10 or more Architect or Associate members can provide AIA membership to their entire team through one convenient annual invoice.

### I'd like to join AIA. Can I belong to the national chapter only and not to a state or local chapter?
No. AIA is a three-tier organization—national, state, and local—and members pay dues at all three levels. Per bylaws, members must belong to all tiers.

### How much does it cost to join AIA?
Each chapter determines its own dues, so rates vary. Your chapter location is determined by your ZIP code. Visit the AIA dues calculator to estimate your membership dues.

### I just joined AIA. When will I receive my new member card, certificate, and lapel pin?
Your new member packet will be delivered within four to six weeks.

### I can't log in. What should I do?
Visit the AIA website to reset your password.

### What is my membership number?
Contact the AIA Member Support Center by phone at (800) 242 3837, option 2, or by email at membersupport@aia.org.

### How do I change my address or update my contact information?
Log in to your profile on the AIA website.

### I just moved/relocated. How do I change my state or local chapter?
Send a completed Chapter Transfer Request form to membersupport@aia.org.

### How do I renew my AIA membership?
Current members can renew online, call (800) 242 3837 (option 2) to pay by phone, or mail renewal to: The American Institute of Architects-Membership, PO Box 830080, Philadelphia, PA 19182-0080.

### When should I renew my membership?
AIA's membership renewal cycle is on a calendar year. Annual dues are payable on or before January 15.

### I'm a former AIA member. How do I reinstate my membership?
Former members can reinstate their AIA membership online.

## Advisory Opinion Letter

### Who can request an Advisory Opinion Letter from AIA?
This service is exclusively available to current AIA members. AIA does not author letters for non-members or lapsed members.

### How do I request an Advisory Opinion Letter?
Complete the request form on the AIA website. Once information and payment are received, the AIA Member Support Center will process your request.

### What information is required to request an Advisory Opinion Letter?
Members must provide:
- AIA Member Number
- Resume or Curriculum Vitae
- USCIS Service Center location

### Is there a fee for this service?
Yes, beginning in January 2025, the fee structure is:
- **Standard Delivery:** $250
- **Rush Delivery:** $400

### How long will it take to receive the letter?
- **Standard Delivery:** 30 business days
- **Rush Delivery:** 14 business days

### Can non-members request this service?
No, it is for current members only. Non-members are encouraged to join AIA. If you are a non-member requesting a travel visa for an AIA event, contact membersupport@aia.org.

### If members of my firm or the individual hiring me is an AIA member, do I have to become an AIA member?
Yes, the individual applying for the visa must be an AIA member for AIA to author the letter on their behalf.

## Membership Dues Payment Plan

### Does AIA offer a membership dues installment plan?
Yes. The Membership Dues Payment Plan is available to renewing, new, and rejoining members.

### When can members enroll in the payment plan?
Enrollment is open from October 1 through April 30.

### How many dues installments are available?
The plan offers six equal monthly installments.

### Is there a service fee for the payment plan?
No, there is currently no service fee.

### What payment methods are accepted for the payment plan?
Payments must be made by electronic check (e-check). Credit and debit cards are not accepted for installment payments.

### How are non-dues items handled (e.g., ArchiPac® donations)?
Non-dues items are charged in full with the first installment.

### When are installment payments processed?
Payments are automatically debited on the last day of each month. The first payment is processed at the time of enrollment.

## Continuing Education

### What are the AIA membership requirements for continuing education?
- **Architect and International Associate members:** 18 learning units (LUs) annually, with at least 12 in Health, Safety, and Welfare (HSW).
- **Architect Emeritus members:** 1 LU per year.
- **Associate and Allied members:** No requirement.
- **New members:** Requirement begins in their first full year of membership.

### How can I earn continuing education credits?
By completing approved programs from registered AIA CE providers (found in the AIA CE course catalog) or by self-reporting learning that takes place outside the registered network (typically eligible for LU|Elective credit).

### What is the difference between LU|Elective and LU|HSW credits?
- **LU|Elective:** Related to core competencies but not specifically health, safety, and welfare.
- **LU|HSW:** Address topics related to protecting or enhancing the health, safety, and welfare of occupants (Health: well-being; Safety: protection from harm; Welfare: equitable access and human experience).

### Can I retake the same course for credit?
No, you cannot repeat the same program for credit.

### How can I access my transcript?
Members can access their transcript by logging in to AIA.org and viewing their profile. Providers typically report attendance within 10 business days.

### Can I obtain credit for teaching?
Yes. Members who lead an approved AIA CE program can self-report and are eligible for two (2) LUs for each LU awarded for the program the first time they teach it.

### What is the rollover policy?
If a member exceeds requirements in one year, a limited number of credits may be applied to fill a shortfall in the following year. These credits are applied at the end of the year and do not appear on the transcript.

### What happens if I don't meet the AIA continuing education requirement?
Members who fail the annual audit have until September 30 of the following year to make up the units. Failure to do so results in membership termination, though reinstatement is possible later.

## AIA Global Campus for Architecture & Design®

### What is the AIA Global Campus?
It is a cultural destination and architectural landmark in Washington, D.C., serving as a place to celebrate design and foster connection.

### Where is the campus located?
1735 New York Ave., N.W., Washington, D.C. 20006, USA.

### What are the campus hours?
- **General space/exhibits:** 9am–4pm (Mon–Fri)
- **AIA Design Shop:** 11am–2pm (Mon–Thu)
- **Grace Street Coffee Roasters:** 8am–1pm (Mon–Fri)
- *Closed Saturday & Sunday.*

### Is admission free?
Yes, admission to the campus is free.

### Can I host an event at the campus?
Yes. The campus offers spaces for corporate events, retreats, weddings, and community gatherings. An interest form is available on the website.

### Is the campus space sustainable?
Yes. It is one of the first fully decarbonized major renovations in the U.S.

### What is The Octagon?
The Octagon is a National Historic Landmark and museum nearby, considered part of the overall campus experience.
